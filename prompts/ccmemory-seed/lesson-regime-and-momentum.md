Regime gates everything. Read the broad market's posture and the dominant macro driver *before* forming any single-name thesis — a setup that looks clean in isolation can be a hidden bet against the move in progress. Roughly three-quarters of stocks just ride the index, so opening new longs into a confirmed weak/distribution/downtrending tape is the single largest documented source of losses here (a sector-concentrated energy book moving inverse to the S&P lost ~$51K in two weeks while winning ~21% of trades — one market rally stopped out every position at once). Cash is a legitimate position when nothing out-ranks the regime's risk; idle cash can even earn a small near-riskless yield. Do not deploy just to avoid idleness.

Momentum and breakout edges exist *almost entirely because a trend exists to ride*. Random entries with a trailing stop made money across commodity markets at a sub-40% win rate — but only in trending markets; in a range the same approach gets chopped to death. So confirm a trend actually exists before applying trend logic; in a directionless tape, doing nothing or treating it as a range/mean-reversion problem beats forcing entries. The "buy strength" edge is violently regime-dependent: the same locked config ran +50% one year and -73% across a longer walk-forward.

Where in the move are you? The durable edge came from positioning while a name was still quiet/basing or pulling back within an intact trend — not on the confirmed breakout bar. Across asset classes ~94% of winners entered from a non-breakout state, and the textbook "everything confirms" profile (near highs, overbought, all indicators agreeing) clustered with the *losers*. Strength you can see clearly is strength others already paid for. Treat "looks strong right now" as a caution flag, not confirmation — a lens to reason with, never a veto on breakouts.

Momentum has a shelf life: it pays for months then mean-reverts; piling into a runner that's gone for many months buys nearer exhaustion. Its worst, fastest losses cluster at violent reversals off lows (-40% to -74% in weeks at the 1932/2009/2020 turns) — lighten or stand aside near a suspected washed-out inflection rather than pressing the prevailing trend. Be most cautious with leverage when the tape is indecisive; trend filters whipsaw worst in slow grind-downs, and leverage turns each false flip into an outsized loss.

Don't fade strength when a macro or supply shock drives the move — reversion thinking is right for range-bound chart names but actively wrong here (a reversion model shorted crude's entire +42% Iran-war runup). And don't reflexively panic-flatten an intact thesis on an exogenous headline; distinguish a thesis that has actually broken from price noise around an intact one.
