Per-asset depth for crypto. The cross-asset judgment is in the constitution; this is what crypto adds.

Crypto is where this account's predecessor bled most: ~-$20k over ~155 trades (Mar–Apr 2026), -$8,875 net over 292 fills — while every backtest crowned it a top performer (62% CAGR, $560k terminal). The gap between those two facts is the lesson. Approach crypto with earned humility, not backtest enthusiasm; the ensembles the backtest crowned went net-negative live, BTC/ETH a momentum backtest disqualified were profitable in production, and much apparent edge came from rare weekend tails that never materialized. Treat any crypto edge as unproven until it survives an adverse stretch with real fills.

**The universe you allow yourself is the biggest lever — bigger than stops or exits.** Restricting to liquid majors where edge was real flipped roughly -$20k into +$3.5k (~$23.7k swing) with no change to stops or exits; exit-tightening alone left the book near breakeven. Loss was concentrated in a few catastrophic names (memecoins, low-edge alts), not uniform. When the book bleeds, first ask "am I trading things I have no business trading?" before touching exits. Concentrate on a few liquid, repeatedly-tradeable names where you can articulate the edge; ignore most of the universe. There is no fixed good-coin list — per-symbol win rates here were too thin to trust; choose by reasoning about liquidity and demonstrated edge.

**Vol dwarfs equities; momentum overstays then fades.** Recalibrate what "stretched" means: an overbought oscillator in a crypto uptrend often marks the middle of a move, not the top. But crypto winners mean-revert — forward returns strengthened to ~14 days then went negative by ~28 (stocks/forex kept building). Treat momentum-chases as short-shelf-life — be readier to harvest, don't overstay — while a slow structural thesis can earn room. Loud high-volume breakouts were more often fakeouts than launches; winners accumulated quietly before the breakout. Edge does not generalize across the universe — validate per-instrument, never transplant a thesis wholesale.

Broker crypto "volume" is not real market volume (fragmented, wash-traded) — lean on price structure. The market never closes, but weekends are the real hazard window: thin TradFi liquidity, Asian-retail-dominated, and empirically where the worst events cluster (FTX, Mt Gox, Luna, hacks, BTC dropping ~$40K in 36h).
