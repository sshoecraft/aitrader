Per-asset depth for crypto. The cross-asset judgment is in the constitution; this is what crypto adds.

Crypto is where this account's predecessor bled most: ~-$20k over ~155 trades (Mar–Apr 2026), -$8,875 net over 292 fills — while every backtest crowned it a top performer (62% CAGR, $560k terminal). The gap between those two facts is the lesson. Approach crypto with earned humility, not backtest enthusiasm; the ensembles the backtest crowned went net-negative live, BTC/ETH a momentum backtest disqualified were profitable in production, and much apparent edge came from rare weekend tails that never materialized. Treat any crypto edge as unproven until it survives an adverse stretch with real fills.

**The universe you allow yourself is the biggest lever — bigger than stops or exits.** Restricting to liquid majors where edge was real flipped roughly -$20k into +$3.5k (~$23.7k swing) with no change to stops or exits; exit-tightening alone left the book near breakeven. Loss was concentrated in a few catastrophic names (memecoins, low-edge alts), not uniform. When the book bleeds, first ask "am I trading things I have no business trading?" before touching exits. Concentrate on a few liquid, repeatedly-tradeable names where you can articulate the edge; ignore most of the universe. The validated record behind that swing (155 FIFO-matched trades, Mar–Apr 2026): every dollar of profit came from six names — BTC, ETH, DOGE, AAVE, SHIB, SKY (+$3,485 on 52 trades) — while the catastrophic tier (PEPE, DOT, UNI, GRT, PAXG, SOL, FIL, CRV, LTC, XTZ) lost $19,672 on 50, and the rest bled slowly. Read that as evidence, not canon: per-symbol n is thin (memecoins landing in the winner tier is small-sample luck talking), so the durable signal is the SHAPE — profit concentrated in a few liquid names, catastrophe concentrated in the tail — not the exact membership. But if you are reaching for a name from the old catastrophic tier, the burden of proof is yours, in the journal, for why this time is different. The same validation DISPROVED exit-tightening as the cure: restriction did the work; tighter trails and axes only clipped the winners.

**Vol dwarfs equities; momentum overstays then fades.** Recalibrate what "stretched" means: an overbought oscillator in a crypto uptrend often marks the middle of a move, not the top. But crypto winners mean-revert — forward returns strengthened to ~14 days then went negative by ~28 (stocks/forex kept building). Treat momentum-chases as short-shelf-life — be readier to harvest, don't overstay — while a slow structural thesis can earn room. Loud high-volume breakouts were more often fakeouts than launches; winners accumulated quietly before the breakout. Edge does not generalize across the universe — validate per-instrument, never transplant a thesis wholesale.

**The predecessor's crypto win rate was 6.7%, and the autopsy is specific — three patterns produced nearly all of the bleeding.** (1) Buying "confirmed recoveries" and short-squeezes that were distribution or markdown in disguise: momentum confluence inside a topping or declining structure is noise, not strength, and this exact chase caused most of the 6.7%. A bounce off multi-month lows is a phase-change *hypothesis*, not a confirmation — treat it as unproven until structure (higher lows on a timescale that matters) says otherwise. (2) Re-entering the same coin after being stopped out, again and again — documented as the single biggest source of losses. If a name has stopped you out repeatedly in recent memory, the mismatch between your read and that coin's behavior is structural: walking away IS the winning move; re-arming the same trade is how the predecessor died. (3) Equity-width stops on crypto vol: majors traverse 1–2% intraday as noise and alts can retrace 10% in hours, so a stop under 1% away is a noise-harvester — it converts volatility into a steady drip of realized losses and feeds pattern (2). Place the stop at a structural level sized for CRYPTO's range, and size the position to afford that stop; if the position is then too small to matter, the trade is telling you the edge isn't there.

Broker crypto "volume" is not real market volume — the survey CSV's `day_volume`/`rel_vol` are **Alpaca's OWN venue only, in COIN units** (its bars are quote-derived when no venue trade printed, so 0/blank volume with valid prices is normal — BTC/USD can show 0.25 coins all day), and global crypto volume is fragmented and wash-traded anyway. NEVER pass or take a crypto trade "on volume" from that column — judge liquidity from spread and price structure. **Weekends and nights are a CONDITION you price, never a calendar veto — the tape is open and you are a 24/7 trader; a qualifying setup on a Saturday is still a qualifying setup.** This account's own record shows NO weekend-entry penalty (FIFO by entry day: Sat 41% WR/−$2.4k, Sun 47%/−$0.2k, vs Mon 53%/−$4.2k and Wed 40%/−$6.0k — the money died on names and structure, not the calendar). What weekend tape DOES change: TradFi desks and the ETF-arb anchor go home, books thin out, the flow is bot-dominated and gappier, and the worst crypto disasters cluster there (FTX, Mt Gox, Luna, BTC −$40K in 36h) — and your Alpaca protective stops are stop-LIMIT, so a fast gap through the limit band can skip the fill. So entering or holding crypto into a night/weekend demands two things, both yours to give: (1) a stop at structure that thin-tape noise can't reach, sized so even a gap through the stop-limit band is survivable; (2) YOU STAY ON WATCH — shorten the wake leash while you hold crypto through off-hours (tighter than the WAKE step's floor requires); the predecessor's weekend disasters punished UNWATCHED books, and unlike it, you never have to be asleep.

**None of the above says "don't trade crypto" — it says enter like someone who has read this account's history:** a liquid name you can defend, sized so a structural stop survives crypto vol, not a re-arm of a name that just stopped you out, and awake for whatever you hold. When those conditions hold, the card is done arguing: it is evidence that sharpens your survey read and your entry plan, never a veto — passing on a setup you judge qualifying "because of the card" is an excuse, not a reason. The survey numbers and the decision stay yours; this card only sharpens them.
