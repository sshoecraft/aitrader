name: lesson-overnight-and-gaps
description: A stop does NOT survive a gap; every overnight/weekend/thin hold carries full distance to the reopen — carry it only for a reasoned thesis.
metadata:
  type: reference
---

A market break — overnight, weekend, holiday, extended hours — is a window where you cannot act and a stop cannot save you. A resting protective stop only works while the tape is continuous and liquid. Across a break, price can reopen well past your stop, so the risk you actually carry is the FULL distance to wherever it reopens, not the distance to your stop. Size and decide as if the stop isn't there for the duration of the break. (TLRY gapped down through an RTH-only stop at the open after a late-day entry was held overnight — a large loss the stop never bounded.)

Keep the stop distinct from a planned exit. A stop bounds downside only while it's live; a planned flatten is a separate action that can fail on its own. Neither should give false comfort about the other.

Match holding period to the thesis, not to any universal clock. If your reason was intraday — today's move, a catalyst resolving by close — then being flat by close IS the thesis, and drifting into an overnight hold silently takes a position you never chose with gap risk you never priced. A long-horizon thesis shouldn't be force-flattened either. Horizon is part of the edge: an open-to-close edge backtested strongly positive gave it all back, turning into a loss with deep drawdown, when held multi-day; a crypto signal showed positive alpha at ~20-60 days but went negative by ~120. Carrying past the window where the edge lives turns a winner into a coin flip.

Break risk is per-asset. Stocks gap every night plus weekends. Forex/futures break mainly Fri-close to Sun-open — an intra-week trend can ride, the weekend is the hazard (Sunday forex opens gapping 50-150 pips; geopolitical futures moves ~-11% next session). Crypto never closes but weekends are thin TradFi liquidity, Asian-retail-dominated, and empirically where the worst events cluster (FTX, Mt Gox, Luna, hacks, exploits, BTC dropping ~$40K in 36h).

What you're pricing is the tail, not the average. The question isn't "what does a typical Friday cost" but "could one catastrophic open hurt the account unrecoverably." A flatten costing a fraction of a point of CAGR that prevents one ruinous Sunday-open gap per several years is positive-EV. Refusing gap risk is itself a legitimate, risk-reducing position. Default question before carrying into a break: what edge justifies eating an unmanageable gap? "I just didn't get around to exiting" is never that answer.

This is judgment, not a fixed flatten clock — no mechanical timestamp rule.
