The prior mechanical system lost money everywhere: 189 trades, -$114k, ~20% win rate, every asset class and every clean subset negative. Treat that record as a map of how accounts die, not a menu of edges. The most account-protective reasoning here is to recognize that most apparent edge dissolves into one of four illusions.

**Survivorship.** Backtests on today's-survivor universes manufacture fake results that cannot be corrected away — a phantom 1.34 Sharpe / 47% CAGR, and once an accounting artifact inflated $50k to $113M. When a result is far better than the benchmark or than the effect could plausibly deliver, presume a data/accounting artifact until proven otherwise.

**Levered or de-risked beta masquerading as alpha.** A "better Sharpe" was usually bought by cutting return (CAGR 15%→5-10%) or sitting in cash ~90% of days; momentum ETFs earned more raw return at *lower* Sharpe. If an apparent win is replicable by simply levering or de-levering the index, it isn't skill. Clean test: would reversing or randomizing the signal earn about the same? Copying Congressional *sells as buys* did as well as copying buys — the "edge" was just large-cap beta.

**Cost.** A statistically real signal can still be uninvestable: thin few-bp edges die at realistic execution/turnover cost, and the more often a thesis demands trading, the more skeptical you should be it survives its own friction. Judge net, not gross.

**Small-sample noise.** With a handful of trades the strongest "signal" rides one lucky outlier — 10 of 19 metrics flipped sign when one row was cleaned.

No single packaged number survived: scores, "confidence," and ranks were non-predictive or *anti*-predictive (highest-scored crypto did worse; a momentum model scored a bear/inverse ETF a top buy). A 0.94-0.97 AUC model separating known winners from losers delivered roughly -12% to -13% annual alpha live — ranking past winners and beating the universe forward are different questions that routinely disagree. When live fills contradict the backtest, the live record wins (crypto ensembles topped every leaderboard yet lost -$8,875 live).

Concluding the evidence doesn't yet support a conviction is a legitimate, often correct finding. Cash is a valid position when nothing out-ranks it.
