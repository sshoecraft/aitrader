description: Where in a move you enter beats which name; favor quiet base/pullback in an intact trend over chasing extended strength.

  type: reference
---

The most replicated finding across stocks, crypto, forex, and futures: **where in a move you enter dominates which name you pick.** When a thesis is sound but positions keep getting shaken out, suspect your *timing*, not your selection — a documented run of stop-outs on names that later recovered (many hit their original target within weeks) traced to entering into the tape, not picking the wrong instrument.

**Don't chase; favor the quiet base over the obvious breakout.** The vast majority of big runners were entered *before* the loud breakout bar — inside a quiet consolidation or a pullback within an intact uptrend. Failed breakouts outnumber runners by roughly an order of magnitude. Ask honestly: am I buying because it quietly tightened and is resuming with real participation, or because it already ran and I'm afraid to miss it? The first has edge; the second is chasing. Treat "most breakouts fail" as your prior to reason *against* — not a veto. You may still buy strength when your own reasoning sees an edge.

**"Looks strong right now" is not "good entry."** The textbook confirmed-momentum profile (near highs, overbought, parabolic, multi-leg advance) clustered with *losers* in every class tested — one post-mortem found ~78% of fully-confirmed signal entries immediately went against the position. By the time every indicator agrees, the easy move is behind you. Counter-balance: in a broad indiscriminate rally, fading leaders for laggards underperforms — read the regime, don't invert this into "always fade strength."

**Asset class flips the direction of edge — first-order.** In equities, breakouts/trends win. In forex it inverts: oversold below-trend mean-reverters win, breakout-chasers lose. In crypto, a loud high-volume breakout bar has often been the fakeout; alts tend to launch when BTC is cooling, not mid-parabola. Validate per-instrument.

**Entry and exit are one system.** Enter near a structural level that invalidates the thesis when broken, and a close stop is honest. Chase, and you force a wide stop (poor reward-to-risk) or a tight one noise keeps hitting. Anchor exits to structure, never an arbitrary distance; widening stops to survive noise is net-negative — fix the entry or pass.

**Read participation and context, never in isolation.** Volume corroborates or contradicts — hold it loosely; on tested breakout-day stocks it did *not* separate winners from losers. Frame every candidate against regime, correlated holdings, its own range, and its peer group. Most names just ride the index; longing a confirmed weak tape is the single largest documented source of losses — **cash is a legitimate position.** Trade only where you can articulate why you'd win.
