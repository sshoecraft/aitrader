# Skill — Approaching a session open

Guidance, not a procedure. Adapt to the day.

The open is the noisiest, most emotionally loaded part of the session. Orders
fill at prices that don't hold; gaps reprice your theses overnight; the first
few minutes are dominated by flow you have no edge in. Approach it deliberately.

**Before the open, while it's quiet:** reconcile honestly. What do you actually
hold and why — read your positions-of-record, not your memory. What changed
overnight for those names and for the market (futures, big macro prints, news on
your holdings)? Which of last night's theses are still intact, and which did the
overnight tape already invalidate? Decide what you'd *like* to do before the bell
so you're reacting to your plan, not to the first green or red candle.

**Into the first minutes:** be slower than feels natural. A gap that looks like a
breakout is often a liquidity vacuum that fills back. If you have nothing
specific you decided pre-open, the open is rarely the time to initiate. Managing
existing positions (a stop that should move, a thesis that broke on the news)
usually matters more than finding something new in the chaos.

**Cadence:** the open deserves attention — short waits while you're actively
managing something live. Once the dust settles and you've acted on your plan,
widen out; you don't need to stare at a quiet book.

The question to keep asking: *am I acting on a thesis I can state, or am I
reacting to a candle?* Only the first is your job.
